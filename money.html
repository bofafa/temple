Attract Prosperity in Tokyo

Gotokuji Temple — home of the lucky cat Maneki-Neko and one of Japan’s most iconic spots for financial wishes.

Gotokuji Temple 豪徳寺

City:Tokyo, Japan
Symbol:Maneki-Neko (Beckoning Cat)
Religion:Buddhism
Address:2 Chome-24-7 Gotokuji, Setagaya City, Tokyo
Opening Hours:6:00 AM - 5:00 PM daily

Legend says a cat once saved a feudal lord from a lightning storm, leading to the founding of Gotokuji. Today, it's a peaceful haven where people offer lucky cats to manifest wealth, success, and good fortune.

How to Make a Wish

  1. Purchase a Maneki-Neko figure from the temple shop.
  2. Write your wish or goal on the cat's base or tag.
  3. Place it gently among the hundreds of other cats on the offering racks.
  4. Bow in gratitude and visualize your financial blessing coming true.

Nearby Attractions

  • Shimokitazawa - explore vintage boutiques, cafés, and indie bookshops nearby.
  • Setagaya Daikan-Yashiki - a preserved Edo-era samurai house around the corner.

Travel Tips

  • Best time to visit: Early spring or autumn for pleasant weather and photo ops.
  • Don’t forget to bring coins for omikuji fortunes or donation boxes.
  • Nearest Station: Gotokuji Station (Odakyu Line)
